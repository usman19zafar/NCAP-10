1. Core architectural insight to bake into NCAP‑10
a) Mutable Turing Tape (MTT)

NCAP‑10 assumes memory is not static RAM but a mutable tape:
rewritable, context‑dependent, interference‑prone.

This directly underpins the Mutable Memory Behavior dimension and partially Dynamic Reasoning.

Tasks: controlled overwrites, partial erasures, context shifts, and recovery challenges.

Utility: separates systems that merely store from systems that manage change in memory.

b) Glial Field (Astrocytes + Oligodendrocytes)

NCAP‑10 treats glial‑like processes as a field, not a side note:

astrocyte‑like: local gain, metabolic support, homeostasis

oligodendrocyte‑like: conduction velocity, timing alignment, myelination analogues

This is the backbone of Glial‑Field Modulation and interacts with Timing, Stability, and Energy Flow.

Utility: evaluates whether a system can self‑regulate under metabolic and timing constraints, not just compute.

c) Digital substrate realism

NCAP‑10 explicitly acknowledges:

you are on a digital computer, not wet biology

so you must exploit what digital does best: precision, speed, reproducibility, large‑scale simulation

The goal is metabolic/physiological analogy, not naive mimicry.

Utility: keeps NCAP‑10 grounded—biologically inspired, digitally optimized.

d) Edge‑weighted graphs are not enough

NCAP‑10 explicitly rejects “graph + weights” as a sufficient model of cognition.

Synaptic Favorability is not just edge weight; it is a function of:

timing

energy

history

glial state

context

Utility: forces richer operators and state variables; prevents trivialization of neuromorphic behavior.
