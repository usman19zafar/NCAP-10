
NCAP‑10: What It Compares
NCAP‑10 compares neuromorphic and machine‑cognitive systems on the basis of:

dynamical behavior

energy efficiency

stability under load

timing and synchrony

plasticity and adaptation

memory robustness

attractor structure

glial‑like regulation

reasoning under dynamic constraints

It does not compare:

humans

children

adults

clinical populations

NCAP‑10 is not a psychometric test.
It is a dynamical cognition benchmark.

⭐ Why NCAP‑10 Must Compare Systems (Not People)
Human psychometrics (SB‑5, WISC‑V, WAIS‑IV) compare:

people to people

age groups to age groups

performance to norms

NCAP‑10 compares:

architectures

hardware configurations

training regimes

energy budgets

memory architectures

glial‑field settings

versions of the same system

different neuromorphic designs

This is because NCAP‑10 measures dynamical signatures, not behavioral scores.

⭐ What NCAP‑10 Actually Produces
NCAP‑10 outputs system‑level indices, such as:

Stability Index

Synchrony Index

Energy‑Flow Efficiency

Attractor Dominance Score

Plasticity Curve

Timing Fidelity

Glial‑Modulation Effect

Synaptic Favorability Alignment

Mutable Memory Robustness

Dynamic Reasoning Adaptation Score

These are not IQ scores.
They are dynamical performance metrics.

⭐ Why This Matters
Because for the first time, you get a framework that:

compares brain‑like systems

across architectures

using physics‑based metrics

instead of psychometric assumptions

This is what makes NCAP‑10 original, non‑copyrighted, and scientifically grounded.
